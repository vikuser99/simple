Growing up, I was always an active person. I enjoyed being outdoors playing different sports and competing with others. Gradually, basketball became my favorite sport after I started watching more NBA games with my dad. We usually watched the popular teams in the Golden State Warriors or the Cleveland Cavaliers, but it was James Harden from the Houston Rockets that stood out the most. My father and I would watch his mixtapes and highlights together, and really enjoyed how he played. It is what inspired me to play basketball in the first place. 

In 3rd grade, I joined my first local basketball league. It was my very first experience playing actual organized basketball, and I realized it was much more different than playing at school or at your driveway. I was always nervous when I had the ball, and that is something I overcame as I played more games. Every practice and game taught me something new, such as playing as a team rather than individually, confidence, etc. As I played more, I started loving basketball a lot more. 

As I kept improving, COVID hit, but I did not allow it to impact my growth. During quarantine I was not able to play basketball much, however I spent a lot of time watching NBA games and analyzing different players I liked. I was learning as much as I could about the game of basketball. My passion never stopped and kept developing. 

When things were back to normal, I joined my club team for middle school. I spent more time practicing and working with my coach after practices or at the park. What challenge I had faced throughout my career was confidence. During practices and scrimmages, I would play freely and score without second guessing myself. Yet in games, I would hesitate a lot and play scared. I knew everything was there, I could hoop, I just needed to put faith in myself and play with my head up. With support from my coach, teammates, and my dad, my confidence started growing. During the winter season, I stopped worrying about making mistakes and played with courage. I started scoring more, and from then I have enjoyed the game more and contend improving every season. From a measly 2 points per game, to 10 points per game. It was huge for my success.

All this momentum carried through high school where I continued to improve and progressed. High school basketball was slightly different though. You had to prove yourself, and earn your minutes. It was not like club basketball where everyone gets equal playing time, we would compete for minutes. That is something I also learned, not everything is granted or given to you in life, you have to work for it. Basketball has taught me much more than how to score or win games. It has taught me confidence, and the value of consistent hard work. Every season brings new challenges and new opportunities to improve, and that is one of the reasons I continue to love the game.
