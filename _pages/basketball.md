# Basketball

Growing up, I was always an active kid. I loved playing all kinds of sports, and one thing I was always known for was my speed. Basketball wasn't my main sport at first, but that started to change when I would watch NBA games with my dad. We usually watched the Golden State Warriors, so the first players I really knew were Stephen Curry and LeBron James.

Everything changed when my parents signed me up for a local basketball league in 4th grade. Even though I had always had a hoop at home and shot around for fun, this was the first time I had ever played organized basketball. I was nervous every game. As soon as I got the ball, I would immediately pass it to someone else because I was too afraid to make a mistake. I didn't score a single point the entire season.

Around that same time, my dad and I started watching James Harden play. He quickly became both of our favorite players. We watched his games, highlights, and mixtapes, and I loved how creative and confident he was with the ball. Watching him made me want to keep playing and improving.

After my first season, I was excited to keep improving, but then COVID hit during 6th grade. Like everyone else, I spent most of my time at home, so I wasn't able to play much organized basketball. Instead, I found myself watching a lot more NBA games and following the league whenever I could. Even though I wasn't on the court as much, my interest in the game kept growing.

When things started getting back to normal in 7th grade, I slowly got back into playing. Then, in 8th grade, I joined my school's basketball club team for the first time. That was when I really started taking basketball seriously and began putting in the work to improve my game.

Confidence was always my biggest challenge. During practices and scrimmages, I could score against my teammates and play freely, but as soon as the game started, I would hesitate to shoot or make plays. It was frustrating because I knew what I was capable of, but I struggled to show it in real games.

With the help of my teammates, coaches, and especially my dad, I slowly became more confident. During the fall season of 8th grade, I didn't score a single point. Then, in the very first game of the winter season, everything seemed to click. I scored around 15 points and finally started playing the way I knew I could. I carried that confidence through the rest of the winter and spring seasons and realized how much believing in myself changed my game.

When I entered high school, I faced another challenge. At first, I wasn't a starter, and I had to prove myself all over again. Through practices, games, and hard work, I earned a starting spot and averaged around 10 points per game.

Since then, basketball has continued to be one of the biggest parts of my life. I've progressed from freshman JV to JV and now varsity, where I mainly play shooting guard while also filling in at point guard when needed. Every season has taught me something new, and I'm still working to improve every day. Looking back, I'm grateful my parents signed me up for that first basketball league because picking up a basketball has become one of the best decisions I've ever made.
